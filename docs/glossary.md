# FREEDOM Framework Glossary

## Key Terms

- **FREEDOM Framework**  
  The overarching proposal consisting of seven interdependent infrastructural pillars designed to deliver universal basic guarantees.

- **Foundation Land**  
  Non-transferable, non-inheritable lifetime land allocation (~2,000 m² baseline in productive areas) provided to every individual at birth for self-sustenance (food, water, shelter, energy). Reclaimed upon death.

- **Equivalent Land Area (ELA)**  
  System that adjusts land allocation size based on local productivity (soil, climate, water, sunlight) so every person receives comparable productive capacity regardless of geographic location. Example: arid regions may receive up to 20,000 m² to equal 2,000 m² in temperate zones.

- **Reserve Settlement System**  
  Pre-designed, multi-tier settlements maintained in readiness for disaster displacement. Used as community resources (education, tourism, research) in peacetime and activated for dignified continuity during emergencies.

- **Universal Social Page**  
  Privacy-by-design digital identity layer. Allows voluntary public sharing while keeping verified attributes sealed except under strict legal authorization. Core to the Mobility pillar.

- **AI Scholar**  
  Advanced AI teaching systems proposed in the Ownership pillar for scalable, personalized, competency-based education available universally and at near-zero marginal cost.

- **Exchange Platform**  
  Universal, decentralized infrastructure for communication, collaboration, trade, and expression. Designed to resist censorship and platform monopolies while protecting personal data ownership.

- **Equilibrium**  
  The pillar guaranteeing comprehensive physical and mental healthcare, sanitation, and access to pharmaceuticals without financial or bureaucratic barriers.

- **ELA-Adjusted Allocation**  
  Foundation Land parcel whose size has been normalized via the Equivalent Land Area model.

- **Structural Guarantee**  
  A right delivered through permanent infrastructure rather than policy or legislation, making denial technically or economically infeasible.

- **18-Year Implementation Pathway**  
  Gradual rollout timeline designed to align with demographic cycles (new generations reaching adulthood) and minimize economic disruption.

- **Legacy Systems**  
  Existing institutions, markets, or bureaucracies (e.g., means-tested welfare, private insurance-heavy healthcare, surveillance-heavy mobility controls) that the framework proposes to phase out or hybridize over time.

- **Dual-Use Infrastructure**  
  Systems designed to serve both everyday needs and emergency/resilience functions (e.g., transport networks that also enable rapid evacuation).

## Additional Concepts

- **Dignified Continuity** — Resilience goal of maintaining habitable, socially connected living conditions rather than mere survival during disruptions.
- **Non-Accumulable Principle** — Foundation Land cannot be bought, sold, or inherited to prevent intergenerational wealth concentration from the baseline guarantee.
- **Privacy-by-Design** — Architecture where individual data is controlled by the user and only disclosed under explicit, auditable legal conditions.
